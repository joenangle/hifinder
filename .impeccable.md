# HiFinder — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary**: audio enthusiasts researching headphone/IEM/DAC/amp purchases — they know terms like "V-shaped", "SINAD", "impedance" and want measurements, rankings, and expert commentary surfaced, not hidden.
- **Secondary**: curious newcomers who don't yet speak that language but want a trustworthy system to recommend a coherent setup within their budget. They should feel *informed, not patronised*.
- **Context of use**: desktop browsers and phones, often in the evening, often while cross-referencing Reddit/ASR/Crinacle in another tab. Sessions are exploratory — users change budget, swap types, compare stacks.

### Brand Personality
- Three words: **informed, opinionated, welcoming**.
- Voice: the tone of a seasoned reviewer writing for *both* fellow enthusiasts and curious newcomers — confident and specific ("best synergy at this price"), but never gatekeeping. When jargon appears, it earns a one-line explanation in place rather than assuming the reader already knows.
- Emotional goals: **trust > delight**. Enthusiasts should feel the recommendations come from rigor; first-timers should feel they've been let in on something, not lectured at.

### Aesthetic Direction
- Editorial / enthusiast-forum lineage — think audio magazines, measurement-site dashboards, review sites that respect the reader's attention.
- Information-dense but legible; numbers and grades are content, not decoration.
- Theme leans **dark**, consistent with evening-listening contexts and ASR/Crinacle visual lineage, but neutrals should be tinted (not flat gray) and accents should be rare and earned.
- **Anti-references**: generic SaaS dashboards (Linear/Vercel clone aesthetic with cyan-on-dark gradients), AI-slop hero-metric layouts, purple-to-blue gradient headlines, glassmorphism cards, side-stripe colored borders on alerts, identical icon-heading-text card grids.

### Design Principles
1. **Data over decoration** — every visual element should encode information (a rank badge, a price delta, a signature tag). If it doesn't, it's a candidate for removal.
2. **Earn every accent** — the accent color is a scarce resource reserved for the primary action and genuinely critical signals; everything else lives in tinted neutrals.
3. **Expert depth always visible, jargon always defined** — Crinacle grades, THD numbers, power calculations should be on the surface, not collapsed; the moment a term lands that a newcomer wouldn't know, a one-line plain-English gloss appears in place. No tooltip-only definitions.
4. **Typographic hierarchy carries the structure** — size and weight contrast do the heavy lifting, not boxes-within-boxes or heavy borders.
5. **Honest states** — loading, empty, and error states should be as considered as the happy path; this is a trust product and users judge us most in friction moments.
6. **One way to say "selected"** — the selected state of any card looks identical regardless of category; category is a label, never a colour repaint.
